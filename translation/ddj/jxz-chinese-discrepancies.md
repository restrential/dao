# JXZ 原文 Discrepancies vs. Wang Bi Edition

Comparison of 江希張's (JXZ) 原文 text in `jxz-chinese-t-punct.md` against the standard Wang Bi edition (王弼本) of the Dao De Jing. All 81 chapters were checked.

**Files compared:**
- JXZ source: `jxz-chinese-t-punct.md` (Traditional Chinese, re-punctuated)
- Reference: 王弼本, sourced from [維基文庫](https://zh.wikisource.org/zh-hant/%E9%81%93%E5%BE%B7%E7%B6%93_(%E7%8E%8B%E5%BC%BC%E6%9C%AC)) and [ctext.org](https://ctext.org/dao-de-jing)

## 4. Notable Scholarly Observations

**Ch. 1 — The 斷句 crux**
JXZ parses 常無慾 and 常有欲 as two discrete noun phrases. Wang Bi (and many modern scholars) read 常無、欲觀其妙 — treating 欲 as the start of the next clause. This is the most debated punctuation in the entire DDJ.

**Ch. 15 — Near-opposite meaning**
JXZ: 故能敝，不新成 — "able to remain worn, not made new"
Wang Bi: 故能蔽而新成 — "through being worn/covered, something new is made"
These carry near-opposite philosophical implications. JXZ's reading emphasizes permanence over transformation. Likely an intentional interpretive stance.

**Ch. 28 — Stanza order**
JXZ's stanza order: 雄/雌 → 白/黑 → 榮/辱
Wang Bi's order: 雄/雌 → 白/**辱** (中間 stanza) → 白/黑
Many scholars consider Wang Bi's middle stanza corrupted (mixing 白 with 辱). JXZ's ordering — which pairs white/black and glory/shame cleanly — may represent a better or independently preserved text.

**Ch. 32 — Unique conclusion**
JXZ ends ch. 32 with 知止所以不始, a conclusion found in no other major DDJ edition. Wang Bi has 知止可以不殆. This may reflect a distinct manuscript family.

**Ch. 55 — Popular audience simplification**
JXZ replaces Wang Bi's detailed creature list (蜂蠆虺蛇: wasp, scorpion, viper, snake) with the generic 毒蟲 (poisonous creatures). This is consistent with JXZ's stated goal of making the DDJ accessible to ordinary readers — a deliberate simplification, not an error.

**Ch. 23 — Structural expansion in 從事於道者 clause**
JXZ reads: 從事於道者：道者同於道，德者同於德，失者同於失
Wang Bi reads: 從事於道者，同於道；德者，同於德；失者，同於失
JXZ inserts 道者 to make the first clause parallel with the second (德者) and third (失者), creating a tidier rhetorical triple. Whether this reflects an older manuscript tradition or JXZ's editorial hand is unclear. JXZ also omits the two final 焉 particles: 信不足焉，有不信焉 → 信不足，有不信.

**Ch. 35 — 泰 vs. 太**
JXZ: 安平泰 — Wang Bi: 安平太
泰 (grand, prosperous) and 太 (greatly, too much) are often treated as interchangeable in classical texts, but 太 is the form found in the Wang Bi base text. This variant is found in several popular editions and may reflect a Qing/Republican-era printing convention.

**Ch. 60 — 亨 vs. 烹**
JXZ: 治大國若亨小鮮 — Wang Bi: 治大國若烹小鮮
烹 (to cook) is the standard character for the famous cooking-small-fish metaphor. 亨 (to receive/offer sacrifice; also a hexagram name in the I Ching) is visually similar and occasionally substituted in manuscripts. JXZ's 字解 renders the character as 烹 (glossing it explicitly as 當烹講), confirming that the intended meaning is cooking, not sacrifice — making this most likely a scribal variant or printing error.

**Ch. 63 — 抱/報 crux**
JXZ: 抱怨以德 — "embrace resentment with virtue"
Wang Bi: 報怨以德 — "repay resentment with virtue"
The 報 reading creates a famous tension with Analects 14.34, where Confucius rejects repaying resentment with virtue. JXZ's 抱 sidesteps this tension. Whether this represents a different manuscript or a deliberate softening is unclear.

**Ch. 81 — 善言/善者 and 辯言/辯者**
JXZ: 善言不辯，辯言不善— Wang Bi: 善者不辯，辯者不善
Substituting 言 (words/speech) for 者 (person/one who) shifts the subject of the claim from people to statements: "good words do not argue" vs. "a good person does not argue." Both readings are philosophically coherent. The 言-form is found in some Han dynasty quotations and may represent an independent manuscript tradition or a deliberate reframing by JXZ for his popular-audience commentary.

## 6. Overall Assessment

JXZ's text is **not a simple Wang Bi reprint**. It represents a distinct textual tradition with the following characteristics:
- Consistent orthographic house style (慾, 衝, 渾, 邦, 歿, etc.) — 衝/沖 pattern now confirmed in chs. 4, 42, 45
- Systematic 人/民 and 邦/國 substitutions suggesting an older or independently preserved manuscript source
- Multiple intentional departures that reflect JXZ's own interpretive stance, particularly in chs. 15, 23, 28, 32, 55, 63, 81
- Minor structural additions (故 in chs. 61, 79; 道者 parallelism in ch. 23; 其 ×2 in ch. 67) that suggest light editorial polishing for clarity
- Possible scribal or printing variants: 亨/烹 (ch. 60), 泰/太 (ch. 35), 不/弗 (ch. 81)
- A small number of likely scribal or OCR errors (chs. 44, 64, 72 ×2)
- Generally higher similarity to Wang Bi than to Mawangdui or Guodian texts, but with divergences comparable to other early 20th-century popular editions

**Chapters confirmed to match Wang Bi exactly** (原文 only, not commentary): 7 (耶/邪 = systematic variant), 22, 25, 33, 43, 56, 57, 70, 71, 73, 79 (aside from 故), 80
